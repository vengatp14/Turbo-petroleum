Petroleum Products

Gas Oil

• Gas oil is a fuel that is cheaper than normal road diesel. Gas oil should never be used in any vehicles on public roads.

• It is the exact same as regular 'white' diesel except it is minimally taxed meaning it's cheaper for the end-user and it is dyed red. So, it is named as ‘Red diesel’.

Naphtha Oil

• Naphtha is created by distilling petroleum, and it's a component of gasoline and kerosene. It is a flammable liquid hydrocarbon mixture.

• Mixtures labelled naphtha have been produced from natural gas condensates, petroleum distillates, and the distillation of coal tar and peat.

Fuel Oil

• Fuel oil, also called furnace oil is the fuel consisting residues from crude-oil distillation. It includes distillates (the lighter fractions) and residues (the heavier fractions). It consists of long-chain hydrocarbons, particularly alkanes, cycloalkanes, and aromatics.

• It is often used as a backup fuel for peaking power plants in case the supply of natural gas is interrupted or as the main fuel for small electrical generators.

Crude Oil

• Crude oil implies a mixture of hydrocarbons that exists in liquid phase in natural underground reservoirs and remains liquid at atmospheric pressure after passing through surface separating facilities.

• It is refined to deliver a wide exhibit of oil-based commodities, including heating oils; gasoline, diesel and jet fuels; lubricants; asphalt; ethane, propane, and butane; and many other products used.

Lubricating Oils

Lubricating oils are a product of the crude oil refining process. The various Properties required of the oil are obtained as a result of blending and the introduction of additives.

• Gear Oils and Greases

In gear lubrication the oil separates metal surfaces, reducing friction and wear.

• Petroleum Waxes

These products are employed in paper manufacture and food stuffs.

Petro Chemicals

Petro Chemicals are derived from a starting material obtained from petroleum. They are virtually identical to the same chemical produced from other sources, such as coal, coke, or fermentation processes.

Aromatics

The aromatic compounds produced in the catalytic reforming of naphtha are major sources of petrochemical products.

Polymers
